When synthesizing information from multiple disparate sources (e.g., .txt, .md, PDF) into a single narrative artifact (like speaker notes or intelligence summaries), the current process relies heavily on ad-hoc prompting. I propose developing and documenting a standardized methodology that dictates: 1) How to prioritize conflicting data points; 2) A consistent structure for extracting Capabilities vs. Benefits; and 3) A mandatory validation step against source metadata. This will improve output consistency across all knowledge creation tasks.
