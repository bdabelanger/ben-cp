# Human Service Subdomains


> **Source:** Confluence — [Human service subdomains](https://casecommons.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/PROD/pages/2460385291/Human+service+subdomains)
> **Last updated in Confluence:** 2022-05-12
> **Status:** ✅ Still relevant — subdomain definitions are stable

The product team focuses research and development on the **bolded** subdomains below. This list is evaluated periodically with Sales and Customer Success.

---

## Priority Subdomains (Active Focus)

**Children, Youth and Family Services**
Children, youth and family services ensure all children and youth live in safe, permanent, and stable environments. Includes out-of-home placements, foster care, kinship providers, group homes, and organizations that work with entire families or solely with children/youth.

**Community Services**
Multi-program providers serving broad geographic areas (towns, counties) across multiple populations — older adults, people with disabilities, victims of disasters, immigrants and refugees, LGBTQ+, veterans, unhoused individuals, job seekers. Includes United Way-type navigation organizations.

**Crime Diversion, Reform & Re-entry**
Case management at any stage of justice involvement — court diversion, pre/post-adjudication, incarceration, re-entry. Services include emotional support, legal system navigation, housing assistance, and advocacy. Interdisciplinary teams including law enforcement, attorneys, correctional officers, and mental health professionals.

**Homeless Services**
Organizations that prevent homelessness, rehouse individuals and families, and minimize trauma caused by homelessness.

**Victims Services**
Support for victims of crime — governmental (police departments) and CBOs. Heavy focus on sexual assault, trafficking, family violence, and stalking. Services include court advocacy, counseling, housing assistance, child care, transportation, crisis intervention, and safety planning.

**Workforce Development & Employment**
Organizations helping youth, students, and working adults achieve educational and career goals while meeting state workforce needs.

---

## Additional Subdomains (Represented but Not Primary Focus)

**Behavioral Health Services**
Organizations providing mental health assessment, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of mental illness, substance use, and addiction. Includes harm reduction and treatment for substance abuse.

**Education**
Early childhood development (0–36 months), early education, specialized education (IEP), school districts supporting children with mental health or social assistance needs, colleges supporting students with barriers to education.

---

## Relevance to Roadmap Themes

- **Theme 1 (Compliance):** Each subdomain has distinct compliance requirements — CJIS for crime diversion, HIPAA for behavioral health, T3C for child welfare (Texas), HMIS for homeless services
- **Theme 2 (Service Models):** Service delivery patterns vary significantly by subdomain — rosters/cohorts for community services, placements for child welfare, bed tracking for homeless services
- **Theme 3 (Workflows):** Approval chains and inter-agency coordination are especially complex in child welfare and crime diversion
- **Theme 5 (Analytics):** Federal reporting mandates differ by subdomain — AFCARS/NCANDS for child welfare, HUD reporting for homeless services, VOCA for victims services
