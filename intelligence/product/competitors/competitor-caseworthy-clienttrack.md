## Overview

CaseWorthy is a US-based, highly-configurable case management platform. In February 2025, CaseWorthy **acquired Eccovia**, makers of the ClientTrack HMIS platform, and unified both products under the CaseWorthy brand. ClientTrack has 20+ years of history in homeless services and HMIS — this acquisition significantly deepened CaseWorthy's position in that segment.

**Customers:** 130+ as of mid-2021 (CaseWorthy proper); ClientTrack has a large HMIS-specific installed base including major CoCs and state agencies
**Hosting:** Microsoft Azure (ClientTrack/Eccovia)
**Pricing model:** Flat rate (Eccovia/ClientTrack); no pricing info available for CaseWorthy proper
**Org size served:** Mid-market (51–1,000 employees) and small-to-midsize nonprofits (11–200 employees) per Software Advice review data — though implementation complexity skews it toward enterprise in practice

## Claim Sourcing & Confidence

### "Similar size to Casebook"
⚠️ **Weak — inference only.** The 130+ customer figure is from Confluence (Revenue team, mid-2021). No current hard number available. "Similar size" is an inference — do not use this claim publicly without a current verified Casebook customer count to compare against. Safer alternative: *"comparable customer count as of 2021."*

### "Skews enterprise"
✅ **Strong — internally sourced.** Directly from Revenue team Confluence SWOT (Dec 2025): *"CaseWorthy is a feature-rich platform, but its complexity in both usage and configuration creates a high barrier to entry. Consequently, it is best suited for enterprise-level customers who possess the internal technical teams and budget required to support a resource-intensive implementation."* Also supported by G2/Capterra reviews noting complex configuration and need for ongoing support.

### "Dominant HMIS share"
🟡 **Supported but overstated.** ClientTrack has a strong, well-documented HMIS footprint (20+ years, major CoC clients including Houston, Atlanta, Dallas, LA County). The word "dominant" is an inference — the sourced claim is "strong HMIS footprint" or "leading HMIS platform." Use the specific client names below as evidence.

### "Eccovia acquisition"
✅ **Strong — public fact.** Announced February 2025, covered publicly. Minnesota HMIS publicly noted the merger will *"eventually lead to significant reporting performance improvements."* Fully defensible.

## Eccovia / ClientTrack Known Customers

These are publicly documented via Eccovia case studies and announcements:

**Homeless Services / CoC:**
- **Coalition for the Homeless of Houston/Harris County** — leveraged data governance and coordinated entry framework with by-name lists to significantly reduce homelessness
- **Partners for HOME** (Atlanta CoC) — high-profile HMIS partnership
- **Housing Forward** (Dallas CoC)
- **Topeka/Shawnee County CoC** — collaborated with local police to enhance care coordination
- **Los Angeles County Housing for Health** — coordinates health and housing services for the nation's most populous county
- **Minnesota HMIS** — statewide HMIS implementation; publicly noted merger with CaseWorthy will bring reporting performance improvements

**Refugee Services:**
- **YMCA of Greater Houston** — manages services for ~15,000 refugee-eligible individuals annually

**Other:**
- **Bridges from School to Work** (Marriott Foundation) — employment services for youth with disabilities across 13 cities nationwide; transitioned from a "cobbled together" database to ClientTrack

- **Georgia Department of Community Affairs** — featured for innovative use of data in human services

## SWOT Summary

**Strengths:** Strong HMIS footprint via ClientTrack acquisition, feature-rich, domain-deep, best-in-class service/outcome tracking, major reference customers across CoCs and state agencies

**Weaknesses:** No API, complex configuration, customizations break after upgrades, enterprise-oriented pricing and implementation, admin UX dated ("outdated Windows console"), video tutorials auto-open intrusively

**Casebook positioning:** CaseWorthy/ClientTrack owns HMIS — that's their turf and a real gap for Casebook in homeless services. Outside HMIS, their complexity and no-API stance are significant weaknesses. Casebook's sweet spot is organizations that want CaseWorthy's depth without CaseWorthy's implementation burden.
