## Overview

Casebook PBC is a Public Benefit Corporation providing "people-centric" software for human services. It is positioned as a leaner, more modern, and more cost-transparent alternative to enterprise incumbents. Reviewers consistently cite its clean interface, responsive support, and accessibility for staff who are stronger in direct service than data administration.

**Market position:** Growing mid-market — leaner and more focused than enterprise competitors
**Pricing:** Starts at $29/user/month — notably transparent vs. enterprise-heavy alternatives
**Public benefit corp:** Mission-aligned structure resonates with nonprofit buyers
**Key differentiator in reviews:** Modern interface + great support vs. "clunky" legacy alternatives

## Compliance Posture — Casebook's Strongest Theme 1 Story

Casebook has the **most comprehensive publicly documented compliance posture** of any platform in this comparison set. This is a genuine differentiator, not just table stakes.

| Framework | Casebook | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| HIPAA | ✅ Certified | External audit verified. Documented at casebook.net/pricing and Casebook Platform Security PDF |
| FedRAMP | ✅ Certified | Government-level security standard — **only platform in comparison set with this certification** |
| CJIS | ✅ Dedicated program | Public-facing CJIS compliance page (casebook.net/cjis). Shared responsibility model: Casebook handles employee background checks + formally executes CJIS Security Addendums with clients |
| SOC 2 Type II | ✅ Certified | Documented via 2025 SOC 2 Type II audit (executive summary available for prospects under MNDA) |
| BAA | ✅ Available | Standard for HIPAA-covered entities |

### What makes Casebook's compliance story distinctive

**FedRAMP** is the standout. No other platform in the comparison set (Apricot, CaseWorthy, Binti, CharityTracker, Penelope) publicly claims FedRAMP certification. This is significant for government agency customers — municipal, county, and state — and for any customer who needs to demonstrate they're using a federally-vetted platform.

**CJIS is a dedicated program**, not just infrastructure compliance. Casebook formally executes CJIS Security Addendums with clients and handles employee fingerprint-based background checks. CaseWorthy is used by CJIS-adjacent government entities (Georgia CJCC) but does not appear to have a formal dedicated CJIS program. Binti explicitly does not claim CJIS certification. Apricot does not market CJIS compliance.

### CJIS compliance checklist (what to verify in any vendor)
1. CJIS Security Addendum — uniform agreement approved by U.S. Attorney General that vendor must sign ✅ Casebook
2. FIPS 140-2 validated encryption — data at rest and in transit
3. Personnel screening — national fingerprint-based background checks for employees with data access ✅ Casebook
4. MFA — mandatory for remote access to criminal justice information

## How Customers Describe Casebook

### What they praise
- Clean, modern interface — "breath of fresh air" vs. older databases
- Responsive customer support — consistently rated higher than Apricot and CaseWorthy
- Notebook feature called out specifically for sensitive case notes
- Customize forms without deep technical skills
- Lower entry-level pricing — accessible for growing nonprofits
- Direct-service staff (not data administrators) can use it effectively

### What they criticize
- Initial setup feels "cumbersome" or "confusing"
- Can feel "click-heavy" for simple documentation tasks
- Initial configuration less intuitive than day-to-day navigation

### Common switching trigger
Reviewers frequently mention switching from **non-specialized tools** (Salesforce, spreadsheets) because direct-service staff can actually use it.

## Honest Weaknesses

- **Initial configuration effort** — setup harder than day-to-day use. Ongoing investment needed in onboarding.
- **Click-heavy for simple tasks** — bulk actions / datagrid roadmap investments are the response.
- **Reporting depth** — Impact Hub (Apricot) and Penelope's 130+ pre-built reports are ahead today. Reveal BI pre-built packages are the roadmap response.
- **HMIS gap** — genuine capability gap vs. CaseWorthy/ClientTrack for homeless services customers.
- **Billing module** — Penelope has dedicated invoicing/payments; Casebook does not.
