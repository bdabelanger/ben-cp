## Federal Reporting & Child Welfare

| Acronym | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| AFCARS | Adoption and Foster Care Analysis and Reporting System | Child-level data on all children in foster care and adopted. States submit semi-annually to the Children's Bureau. |
| NCANDS | National Child Abuse and Neglect Data System | Voluntary data collection on child abuse/neglect reports from all 50 states + DC + PR. |
| NYTD | National Youth in Transition Database | Collects data on foster care youth and outcomes for those who aged out. Submissions began 2011. |
| CFSRs | Child & Family Services Reviews | Periodic federal reviews of state child welfare systems. States develop Program Improvement Plans (PIPs) in response. |
| CCWIS | Comprehensive Child Welfare Information System | Federally-supported case management system for title IV-E agencies. More favorable federal reimbursement than non-CCWIS systems. |
| SACWIS | Statewide Automated Child Welfare Information System | Legacy predecessor to CCWIS. |
| TACWIS | Tribal Automated Child Welfare Information System | Tribal equivalent of SACWIS. |
| Title IV-E | The Federal Foster Care Program | Open-ended entitlement allowing states to claim federal reimbursement for eligible children in licensed foster homes, institutions, or group homes. |
| Title IV-A | Emergency Assistance / TANF | Block grants for temporary assistance to needy families. Funds clothing, non-medical counseling, homemaker services for up to 120 days. |
| DCS | Indiana Department of Child Services | Casebook's first major state implementation partner. |

## Homelessness

| Acronym | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| HMIS | Homeless Management Information System | HUD-mandated system for homeless services agencies. Data collected by Continuums of Care. |
| CoC | Continuum of Care | HUD program promoting community-wide planning for homelessness. [More info](https://www.hudexchange.info/programs/coc/) |

## Relevance to Roadmap Themes

- **Theme 1 (Compliance):** CCWIS, SACWIS, AFCARS, NCANDS, CFSRs, Title IV-E are all federal compliance/reporting frameworks relevant to child welfare customers
- **Theme 2 (Service Models):** AFCARS and NYTD define specific data elements that must be captured in service delivery for child welfare agencies
- **Theme 4 (Interoperability):** HMIS is a system-level interoperability requirement for homeless services customers (see competitor profiles)
- **Theme 5 (Analytics):** NCANDS, CFSR, NYTD drive specific reporting requirements that customers need Casebook to support
