## Overview

CharityTracker (by Simon Solutions) is a **community-wide coordination network** — fundamentally different in architecture from general-purpose case management platforms like Casebook, Apricot, or CaseWorthy. Rather than serving a single large agency, CharityTracker links multiple local organizations (churches, food pantries, nonprofits) into a **shared database** to prevent "double-dipping" and coordinate aid across an entire community.

It is deployed primarily through **networks** — a United Way or ministerial alliance creates a network and all participating providers, volunteers, and clients share a single tenant. This network model is a structural competitive advantage that general-purpose case management tools can't easily replicate.

**Market position:** Budget-friendly collaborative hub — community coordination, not deep case management
**Starting price:** $20/month, no setup fees — the most accessible price point in the space
**User base:** 1,500+ cities per Crozdesk; organizations as varied as Salvation Army, Red Cross, food pantries, churches, United Way
**Primary differentiator:** Network model + price + simplicity for non-technical staff and volunteers

---

## Why Charity Tracker Is on the Watch List

CharityTracker is the competitor Casebook is most likely to **lose to silently**. At $20/month with no setup fees, it wins on price before a sales conversation even begins. Its network model means an entire community of small organizations can be onboarded at once — the United Way adopts it and every partner agency follows. By the time Casebook is in a deal, CharityTracker may already be embedded.

It is **not a sophisticated product** — it lacks true case management, advanced reporting, and modern UX. But for organizations that primarily need to coordinate aid across a community and track simple client interactions, it does the job at a price point that's hard to argue with.

## Customer Profile (Per G2, Capterra, GetApp, Software Advice)

**Most common organization types (46% nonprofit org management):**
- Benevolence programs & ministries (churches, ministerial alliances managing financial assistance for rent/utilities)
- Small to mid-sized nonprofits: 2–50 employees primarily
- Food pantries & banks (barcode scanning for distribution and meal program attendance tracking)
- Disaster recovery groups (multi-agency coordination after events like hurricanes)

**Typical user profile:**
- Volunteers and non-technical staff — the product is explicitly designed for people who don't have tech skills
- Organization administrators managing a community network

## SWOT Summary

**Strengths:** Lowest price point in the market ($20/month, no setup fees), network model creates community-wide lock-in, purpose-built for human services, HIPAA compliant, includes uncommon features (barcodes, digital signatures, SMS broadcasting, kiosk mode), trains quickly for non-technical volunteers

**Weaknesses:** Outdated software (legacy reporting module), limited configurability, updates focused on bug fixes not features, not a true case management tool (lacks caseworker assignment, open/closed status), data fragmentation across network partners, no meaningful integrations

**Casebook positioning:** CharityTracker wins on price and simplicity for community coordination use cases. Casebook wins on depth, modern UX, real case management, and reporting. The risk is losing to CharityTracker *before* a conversation happens — especially in United Way and faith-based networks where CharityTracker is already embedded. When you're in a deal against them, lead with the gap: "CharityTracker tracks aid. Casebook manages cases."

## Key Differentiator for Sales

> *"CharityTracker tracks aid. Casebook manages cases."*

This single line frames the decision correctly. CharityTracker is a coordination and duplication-prevention tool. It is not equipped to handle case plans, worker assignment, outcome tracking, service enrollment, or compliance reporting. Organizations that start with CharityTracker and grow quickly hit the ceiling fast.
