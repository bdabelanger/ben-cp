# Competitor Profile: Bonterra / Apricot

> **Scope:** Full competitive profile across all 6 roadmap themes.
> **Sources:** Confluence Revenue Team analysis (last updated Dec 2025), Client Portal PRD competitive section, G2/Capterra/GetApp/Software Advice aggregated reviews, Bonterra website, prospect signals
> **Last Updated:** 2026-04-30
> **Status:** 🟢 Solid — some gaps remain on AI roadmap and pricing tiers

---

## Overview

Apricot (by Social Solutions, now Bonterra) is considered Casebook's **primary competitor** with a highly mature, established feature set. Bonterra is the **second-largest social good software company globally**, serving over 20,000 customers including roughly half of the Fortune 100 through its broader portfolio (CyberGrants, EveryAction, Social Solutions legacy products). Apricot is their core case management product.

Bonterra has acquired multiple products (Apricot, ETO, Penelope/Athena) and is consolidating under the Bonterra brand. Within the nonprofit case management space specifically, Apricot is the market leader by footprint. Their marketing focus is heavily on reporting capabilities and outcomes measurement, and their mid-funnel content library is extensive.

**Market position:** Second-largest social good software company globally (Bonterra parent)
**Apricot customer base:** ~74% nonprofit organization management per G2/Capterra review data
**B-Corp status:** Certified 2021
**Primary product positioned:** Apricot 360
**Enterprise tier:** Dedicated enterprise offering for high-volume, multi-service organizations with complex site hierarchies

## Claim Sourcing & Confidence

### "Largest market share" / "Primary competitor"
✅ **Strong.** Bonterra serves 20,000+ customers across its portfolio. Apricot specifically is the most-reviewed nonprofit case management platform on G2 and Capterra. Internal Revenue team Confluence analysis (Dec 2025) designates Apricot as Casebook's primary competitor with a "highly mature and established product." Fully defensible.

### "Enterprise-scale / skews larger orgs"
✅ **Strong.** Bonterra offers a dedicated Enterprise Tier. Known customers include City of Philadelphia Office of Public Safety (municipal government), Catholic Social Services Anchorage (largest homeless services provider in Anchorage), and large agencies managing 30,000+ clients. Reviewers describe it as a "centralized database" for organizations with dozens of distinct programs. Source: Bonterra website + G2/Capterra/GetApp review data.

### "Stagnant innovation / no AI"
✅ **Strong — internally sourced.** Revenue team Confluence SWOT (Dec 2025) explicitly states: *"Limited innovation, with recent releases focused primarily on maintenance and bug fixes"* and *"Lacks modern, high-tech features such as AI-driven functionality."* Also supported by Capterra customer reviews.

### "12–14 months to value"
✅ **Strong — direct customer testimonial** captured in Revenue team Confluence page.

### "Overlaps with Casebook ICP"
✅ **Strong.** Per G2/Capterra/Software Advice review data, most active reviewers are Small Businesses (under 50 employees) and Mid-Market (51–1,000 employees) — directly overlapping Casebook's ICP of sub-40-employee nonprofits.

## SWOT Summary

**Strengths:** Largest market share in space, mature and established, best-in-class audit logs/search/forms/workflows, Impact Hub visual reporting, no-code form building, enterprise tier for large agencies, 20,000+ Bonterra parent customer base

**Weaknesses:** Limited innovation (maintenance/bug fixes focus), explicitly no AI features, expensive, increasingly complex and unwieldy, 12–14 months to value (customer testimonial), requires ongoing support to configure and maintain, double data entry structural problem

**Casebook positioning:** Apricot owns the market on breadth and brand. Casebook's opening is innovation velocity (AI, portal, modern UX), simplicity at ICP scale (sub-40 employees don't need enterprise tiers), and time-to-value. Apricot takes 12–14 months to deliver value; Casebook should be able to make a strong contrast case here.
