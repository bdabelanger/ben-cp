## Core ICP Statement

A **growing human services organization** with **the right decision-makers** in place that is **flexible, open to change, and willing to adapt existing processes** to **align with Casebook's best practices**, including all required and mandated elements.

---

## Quantitative Profile (Revenue Team)

- **Geography:** US & Canada-based (excluding Quebec). International MQLs go to Director of Sales for distribution.
- **Size:** Under 40 employees, over 2 years in existence
- **Technical maturity:** Mid-to-high level of technical knowledge
- **Goals:** Looking for a central repository of information to save time, establish process maturity, improve data fluency
- **Outbound floor:** No minimum company size, but outbound activities should avoid organizations with 5 or fewer users
- **Sectors:** Non-profits and State Government

## Why This Matters for Competitive & Roadmap Decisions

The ICP is meant as a guide for prioritizing **what we build, what we sell, and how we support customers**.

### Reconciling ICP with roadmap themes

- **Theme 1 (Compliance):** SOC 2 + HIPAA cover most ICP segments. CJIS matters for re-entry / police-based social work. T3C matters for foster care / kinship customers. **VOCA is explicitly outside ICP** — confirms our decision to keep VOCA in Theme 2 as a recognized gap rather than a strength.

- **Theme 2 (Service Models):** AFCARS matters for child welfare ICP segments. Residential services (which need bed tracking, complex rosters) are explicitly outside ICP — useful framing when prospects bring up bed tracking complexity.

- **Theme 4 (Interoperability):** ICP includes homelessness services where HMIS is a real concern. The "no API" weakness in CaseWorthy is meaningful here — Casebook's API/Zapier story is a differentiator with mid-technical-maturity ICP customers.

- **Theme 5 (Analytics):** Mid-to-high technical maturity buyers expect real reporting capability — but they're not full-time analysts. This validates the pre-built reports strategy.

- **Sub-40 employees:** Confirms positioning against Apricot/CaseWorthy/ETO (all enterprise-skewing). Casebook's simpler implementation and modern UX are fit-for-purpose for this size.

### Reconciling ICP with competitor positioning

- **Apricot (Bonterra):** Enterprise-skewing, complex, 12–14 month time-to-value. Mid-market ICP customers under 40 employees can't afford that ramp.
- **CaseWorthy/ClientTrack:** "Best suited for enterprise-level customers who possess the internal technical teams and budget required to support a resource-intensive implementation" (their own positioning per Revenue analysis). Outside Casebook's ICP.
- **ETO:** Enterprise legacy with complex implementation. Now sunsetting. Active displacement opportunity for ICP customers.

## Out of Scope Signals (Disqualification Cues)

- Residential service programs (bed tracking, complex licensing) → loop in SCs first
- Heavy VOCA reporting requirements → loop in SCs first
- Quebec-based organizations
- Sub-5-user prospects (for outbound)
- Inflexible organizations unwilling to adapt processes to Casebook's best practices

The current ICP reflects the product as of early 2026 — as Outcomes Tracking, Client Portal, AI features, and improved Services models ship through 2026, the ICP may broaden (e.g., into segments currently disqualified due to capability gaps).
