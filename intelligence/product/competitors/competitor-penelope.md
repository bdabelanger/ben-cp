# Competitor Profile: Penelope (Bonterra / Athena Software)

> **Scope:** Full competitive profile across all 6 roadmap themes.

---

## Overview

Penelope (formerly by Athena Software, now Bonterra Penelope) is a **clinical and health-focused case management platform** — the third distinct product in the Bonterra portfolio alongside Apricot and ETO. It is still a live, actively supported product but is widely characterized as aging and falling behind competitors on modern web features.

Penelope occupies a different niche than Apricot: where Apricot is Bonterra's generalist flagship, Penelope is their **specialized clinical option** — strong in behavioral health, counseling, and health-network documentation. As of late April 2026, Bonterra is migrating Penelope's help content to its centralized Help Center, consistent with its broader portfolio consolidation.

**Market position:** Specialized clinical tool — behavioral health, counseling, health networks
**Target size:** 25+ users — mid-to-large nonprofits and government
**Key differentiator:** Clinical documentation, billing, scheduling — not general human services
**Status:** Active but aging — "falling behind competitors" per reviewer sentiment
**Notable:** 130+ pre-built reports; Tableau integration for advanced analytics

---

## Why Penelope Is on the Watch List

Penelope is relevant for three reasons:

1. **It's another Bonterra product** — prospects evaluating Bonterra's ecosystem may land on Penelope instead of Apricot, particularly if they have clinical or behavioral health programs. Understanding all three Bonterra products helps Casebook navigate those conversations.

2. **Its reporting depth is notable** — 130+ pre-built reports is a specific, citable number that exceeds what Casebook offers today. Casebook's Reveal BI pre-built packages strategy is the roadmap response.

3. **Its weaknesses are Casebook's strengths** — aging UX, steep learning curve, rigid reporting requiring Tableau workarounds, and "clunky" interface are reviewer themes that map directly to Casebook's clean interface and support quality advantages.

## Customer Profile (Per Capterra, Software Advice)

**Most common organization types:**
- Health and behavioral health networks (counseling centers, mental health clinics — primary use case)

## SWOT Summary

**Strengths:** 130+ pre-built reports (best in class), strong clinical documentation and billing, group services with individual-specific notes, mobile fieldwork (Penelope Mobile), configurable forms, government security standards, Tableau integration

**Weaknesses:** Aging interface ("platform from the 90s"), steep learning curve, rigid built-in reporting requiring Tableau workarounds, one-way calendar sync (Outlook only), communication replies not captured in-app, falling behind on modern web features, collaboration tools confusing, no AI features

**Casebook positioning:** Penelope's depth is real — especially in clinical documentation, billing, and pre-built reports. But it's a specialist tool for behavioral health and clinical programs, not a fit for Casebook's broader ICP. Where they overlap, Casebook wins on modern UX, support quality, two-way calendar sync, and innovation velocity. The 130+ pre-built reports gap is the most concrete area to watch and close.
