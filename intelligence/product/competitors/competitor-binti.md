## Overview

Binti is a for-profit, mission-driven tech company specializing in **child welfare and foster care licensing**. It is a vertical specialist — not a general-purpose human services platform — built specifically to streamline the approval process for foster parents and manage placements. It is used by over **550 agencies across 36 states**, managing **nearly half of all children in the U.S. child welfare system**.

Binti is the **AI leader in the human services software space** as of 2026, having integrated Anthropic's Claude to help social workers draft paperwork from recorded meetings — reducing completion time from weeks to hours. This is not a roadmap item; it is shipping.

**Market position:** Specialized vertical leader — child welfare and foster care
**Key customers:** State and county agencies (government-level, not nonprofit-first)
**Scale:** 550+ agencies, 36 states, ~half of U.S. child welfare system
**Funding:** Venture-backed, Silicon Valley
**Monthly releases:** Active product development culture

## Why Binti Is on the Watch List

Your Revenue team's own SWOT explicitly flagged: *"Binti's recent investment in (seemingly) functional AI technology should be a worry, especially if they start opening up to new markets."*

This is the core concern. Binti is niche today — foster care and child welfare. But:
- They have real AI features shipping now (Claude integration)
- They have state-level government relationships at scale
- Their mobile-first, modern UX is a direct contrast to legacy systems
- They are actively expanding (Washington State, New Mexico state contracts)
- Their ICP (child welfare, foster care, kinship) directly overlaps Casebook's ICP

If Binti expands laterally into adjacent human services segments — which is the stated risk — they arrive with AI capabilities, state-level credibility, and a modern product that Casebook would need to compete against directly.

## SWOT Summary (Revenue Team + External Sources)

**Strengths:** AI leader in space (Claude integration, live), best-in-class foster care licensing, mobile-first, modern UX, state-level scale (36 states, 550+ agencies), active monthly releases, API capability, CCWIS compliance

**Weaknesses:** Narrow vertical focus (child welfare only), limited configurability vs. general platforms, lacks donor management / fundraising tools, pricing ranges widely ($667–$439,500 depending on contract)

**Casebook positioning:** Today, Binti is not a head-to-head competitor — they don't serve the same ICP segment beyond child welfare overlap. The risk is lateral expansion. If Binti moves into community services, victim services, or workforce development, they arrive with AI features, state credibility, and a modern product. Casebook needs to ship AI features faster than Binti expands horizontally.
