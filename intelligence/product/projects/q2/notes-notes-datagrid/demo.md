# Demo Guide: Notes — New Authoring Experience + Datagrid

## Canonical Demo Case

**Kelsey Moore** — Lighthouse Child & Family Placements

Kelsey (10) is a foster child placed with the Schumacher family two months ago following removal from her biological mother's care. The Schumachers are committed foster parents with two biological children, Kyle (8) and Jack (3). Kelsey has been bonding with Kyle over art projects. The caseworker is documenting monthly home visits and coordinating trauma-informed play therapy through Lighthouse's in-house clinical team.

**Why this case works:** It generates a natural volume of notes (monthly visits, service notes for play therapy, sibling support group), and the Service Note with multiple people tagged (Kelsey, Kyle, Sarah) shows the tabbed authoring experience at its best.

---

## QA Setup

- Kelsey Moore has an open case with 3–4 existing notes — monthly home visit notes across recent months
- At least one existing Service Note linked to play therapy enrollment
- Sarah Schumacher linked as a household member / caregiver on the case
- Kyle Schumacher linked as a related person so he can be tagged in service notes

---

## Part 1 — New Authoring Experience

Open Kelsey's case and navigate to Notes.

*"Let's document today's home visit. This is the new authoring experience — it looks different, but everything you're used to is still here."*

Create a new General Note. Walk through the consistent top section — date fields, subject, narrative, people involved.

*"Every note type now starts the same way. General Notes, Service Notes — same fields up top, including standardized date handling."*

Switch to a Service Note. Show the tabbed bottom section — add the play therapy service, tag Sarah as the person present, add a brief personal note.

*"For Service Notes you get this tabbed section below — add one service or several, tag who was involved, add personal notes per person."*

---

## Part 2 — The Notes Datagrid

Navigate back to the Notes list.

*"Now let's look at how you find and review notes across this case."*

Show 5 rows minimum visible — *"All your notes, right here. No clicking 'View all' to get to them."*

Sort by Start Date to show chronological visit history.

Quick filter — type "home visit" to isolate monthly visit notes instantly.

---

## Part 3 — Best Practices

*"Same principles as the Service Plan datagrid — a few things worth building into your habit."*

Hide the Author column — *"If you're the only caseworker on this case, you don't need that column. Hide it."*

Pin the most recent home visit note — *"Keep the note you're actively working from pinned to the top."*

Print — *"Print in Notes exports to a readable PDF — great for court documentation or supervisor review."*

*"Any layout changes you make are yours. Your colleague's view is untouched. Come back tomorrow on any device and it's exactly how you left it."*

**Admin note:** There is no admin-level default layout — if your organization wants staff to work from a consistent column setup, you'll need to train them to configure it themselves. The good news is it only takes a minute and sticks permanently.
